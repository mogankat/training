# Car Engine Replacement Framework

## Phase 1: Assessment & Preparation
- Confirm engine replacement is necessary (vs. rebuild or repair)
- Identify replacement engine (OEM, remanufactured, or used) and verify compatibility
- Gather service manual/torque specs for the vehicle
- Estimate labor time and acquire all required parts, gaskets, and fluids

## Phase 2: Tools & Materials
- Engine hoist and load leveler
- Socket/wrench set (metric and standard), torque wrench
- Drain pans, jack stands, floor jack
- Replacement engine, motor mounts (if worn), accessory belts
- Fresh coolant, engine oil, and all new gaskets/seals

## Phase 3: Disassembly
1. Disconnect battery (negative terminal first)
2. Drain coolant, engine oil, and transmission fluid (if applicable)
3. Remove hood for access
4. Label and disconnect all electrical connectors, sensors, and grounds
5. Remove intake, exhaust manifolds, and related plumbing
6. Disconnect cooling hoses, heater hoses, and fuel lines
7. Remove AC compressor, alternator, power steering pump (set aside without disconnecting lines if possible)
8. Detach transmission from engine (support transmission separately)
9. Unbolt motor mounts
10. Attach engine hoist, verify balance, and lift engine clear of bay

## Phase 4: Engine Swap
1. Transfer all ancillary components to new engine (brackets, sensors, flywheel/flexplate)
2. Inspect and replace gaskets, seals, and wear items on new engine
3. Lower new engine into bay, align with motor mounts
4. Torque motor mount bolts to spec
5. Reconnect transmission

## Phase 5: Reassembly
1. Reinstall accessories (AC compressor, alternator, power steering pump)
2. Reconnect all coolant and heater hoses
3. Reconnect fuel lines
4. Reconnect all electrical connectors, sensors, and grounds
5. Install new belts and hoses as needed
6. Reinstall intake and exhaust components
7. Reinstall hood

## Phase 6: Fill & Pre-Start Checks
- Fill with fresh engine oil (correct grade and quantity)
- Fill coolant system and bleed air if required
- Check transmission fluid level
- Verify no loose connections, tools, or rags left in engine bay
- Reconnect battery

## Phase 7: Start-Up & Break-In
1. Crank engine without starting to prime oil pressure (pull fuel pump fuse)
2. Start engine and monitor for leaks (oil, coolant, fuel, exhaust)
3. Check for fault codes with OBD-II scanner
4. Verify correct idle, temperature, and charging voltage
5. Road test with light load for first 500 miles (break-in period)
6. Change break-in oil at 500 miles; inspect for leaks

## Phase 8: Final Verification
- Re-torque accessible fasteners after heat cycles
- Recheck all fluid levels
- Clear any learned adaptations in ECU if required
- Document completed work and new engine serial number
